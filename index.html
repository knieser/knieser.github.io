Kenneth J. Nieser

PhD Student in Epidemiology

I am a PhD Student in the Department of Population Health Sciences at the University of Wisconsin-Madison School of Medicine and Public Health.

I have broad interests in how social and structural factors shape health outcomes and generate health disparities. My emphasis is on mental health and health-related behaviors. Substantive research questions in these areas often present methodological challenges related to model building and latent variable analysis. I focus on developing quantitative methods that can, ideally, improve models and inferences. These approaches incorporate techniques from statistics, applied math, and data science, as well as drawing on methodological developments in other areas of social science.
